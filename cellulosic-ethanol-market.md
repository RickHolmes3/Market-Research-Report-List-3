<p><p>Cellulosic ethanol can be produced from various types of feedstock, with the main market types being corn stover, sugarcane straw and bagasse, and others. Corn stover, the leftover stalks and leaves from corn crops, is a common feedstock for cellulosic ethanol production in regions with large corn cultivation. Sugarcane straw and bagasse, the fibrous residue from sugarcane processing, are also popular feedstocks in regions with significant sugarcane production. The "others" category includes feedstocks such as switchgrass, wood chips, and agricultural residues.</p></p>
