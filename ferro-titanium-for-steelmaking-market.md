<p><p>Ferro Titanium (FeTi40 and FeTi70) are types of alloys primarily used in steelmaking. FeTi40 consists of approximately 40% titanium and is commonly employed to improve the strength and durability of steel. On the other hand, FeTi70 contains around 70% titanium and is preferred when higher levels of tensile strength and heat resistance are required in steel production. Both variants enhance the quality and performance of steel by reducing impurities, enhancing corrosion resistance, and promoting desirable metallurgical properties. These alloys are vital components in the steel industry and contribute to the production of high-quality, robust steel materials.</p></p>
