<p><p>The technologies used for food safety testing can be categorized into several types, including pathogens, pesticides, GMOs, toxins, residues, and others. Pathogen testing involves the identification of harmful bacteria, viruses, or other microorganisms that may cause foodborne illnesses. Pesticide testing is conducted to detect the presence and levels of chemical pesticides used in agricultural practices. GMO testing ensures the accuracy of labeling by identifying genetically modified organisms. Toxin testing involves the detection of various harmful substances such as mycotoxins or heavy metals. Residue testing is performed to determine the presence of veterinary drugs or environmental contaminants in food products. Other types of food safety testing may include allergen testing or authenticity testing for verifying the origin or quality of food items.</p></p>
