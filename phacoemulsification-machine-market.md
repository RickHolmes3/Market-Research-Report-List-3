<p><p>Phacoemulsification machine is a medical device used in cataract surgery to remove the clouded lens and replace it with an artificial intraocular lens (IOL). The procedure involves using ultrasound energy to break up the clouded lens into small pieces which are then suctioned out through a small incision.</p><p>The phacoemulsification machine consists of a console that controls the ultrasound energy and suction, a handpiece with a vibrating tip, and various accessories required for the procedure. The machine offers precision and control, ensuring minimal damage to the surrounding tissue and quicker recovery for the patient.</p><p>The global phacoemulsification machine market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. Factors such as the rising prevalence of cataract, an aging population, technological advancements in surgical techniques, and increasing awareness about eye disorders are driving market growth. Additionally, the high success rate and reduced risk of complications associated with phacoemulsification surgery further boost the demand for these machines.</p><p>In terms of trends, there is an increasing preference for miniaturized and portable phacoemulsification machines that offer ease of use, mobility, and cost-effectiveness.
