<p><p>SNP genotyping and analysis refer to the methods used to detect and analyze single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs), which are variations in a single nucleotide within a DNA sequence. There are different market types for SNP genotyping and analysis, including dynamic allele-specific hybridization, molecular beacons, and SNP microarrays. Dynamic allele-specific hybridization involves using probes that are specific to different alleles of a SNP. Molecular beacons are fluorescent probes that bind to specific SNP sequences. SNP microarrays use DNA chips to identify and analyze large numbers of SNPs simultaneously.</p></p>
