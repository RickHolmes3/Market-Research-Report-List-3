<p><p>Electroencephalography (EEG) devices are used to measure the electrical activity of the brain, primarily to diagnose and monitor brain disorders such as epilepsy and sleep disorders. These devices consist of electrodes placed on the scalp that detect and record the brain's electrical signals. </p><p>On the other hand, electromyography (EMG) devices are used to measure the electrical activity of muscles. They are commonly used in sports medicine, rehabilitation, and neurology to assess muscle function and diagnose conditions such as muscle disorders and nerve damage. EMG devices involve the placement of electrodes on the skin above the muscles of interest, which detect and record the muscle's electrical signals.</p></p>
