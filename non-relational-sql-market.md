<p><p>Non-relational SQL market types include key-value stores, document databases, column-based stores, and graph databases. Key-value stores provide a simple structure of keys and values for quick data retrieval. Document databases store data in flexible JSON-like documents, allowing easy modification and scalability. Column-based stores organize data into columns for efficient querying and high performance. Graph databases focus on relationships between data entities and are suitable for complex interconnected data. These different market types cater to diverse database needs and offer specialized features and benefits for specific use cases.</p></p>
