<p><p>The automotive antivibration mounting market mainly consists of two types: rubber and polyurethane. Rubber mountings are commonly used in vehicles due to their excellent vibration isolation properties. They absorb and dampen vibrations, preventing them from being transferred to the rest of the vehicle. Polyurethane mountings, on the other hand, offer greater durability and resistance to environmental conditions. They provide better stability and control, making them suitable for heavy-duty applications. These mountings play a vital role in reducing noise, improving comfort, and enhancing the overall performance of vehicles.</p></p>
