<p><p>Optical encoders are devices used for converting angular or linear position of a mechanical component into an electrical signal. There are two main types of optical encoders - incremental encoders and absolute encoders. Incremental encoders provide relative position information in terms of pulses, while absolute encoders provide precise position information without the need for a reference point. Both types have various applications in industries such as robotics, automation, and aerospace. The market for optical encoders is driven by the demand for high precision and reliability in position sensing technology.</p></p>
