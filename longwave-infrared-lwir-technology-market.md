<p><p>Longwave Infrared (LWIR) Technology refers to the technology used for detecting and capturing thermal radiation in the longwave infrared spectrum. There are two types of LWIR technology markets: cooled technology and uncooled technology. Cooled technology utilizes a cooling mechanism, such as cryogenics, to lower the temperature of the infrared sensor, resulting in higher sensitivity and better image quality. Uncooled technology, on the other hand, does not require any cooling mechanism and relies on microbolometers to capture infrared radiation. Uncooled technology is generally less expensive but offers lower sensitivity and image quality compared to cooled technology.</p></p>
