<p><p>The antibacterial drugs market includes various types of drugs that are used to treat bacterial infections. Some common types include cephalosporins, penicillins, fluoroquinolones, macrolides, carbapenems, and others. Cephalosporins are antibiotics derived from fungi that work by breaking down bacterial cell walls. Penicillins are a group of antibiotics that inhibit the growth of bacteria by preventing the formation of their cell walls. Fluoroquinolones are synthetic antibiotics that interfere with the replication of bacterial DNA. Macrolides are a class of antibiotics that inhibit the production of bacterial proteins. Carbapenems are antibiotics that inhibit the growth of bacteria by disrupting their cell wall synthesis. Other types of antibacterial drugs may have different mechanisms of action or target specific bacteria.</p></p>
