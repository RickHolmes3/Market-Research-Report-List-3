<p><p>Inorganic coagulants are chemicals like aluminum sulfate or ferric chloride, which work by forming insoluble particles that bind together impurities in water, making it easier to remove them through filtration. Organic coagulants are typically natural polymers like chitosan or polyacrylamides, which have a similar mechanism of action but are derived from organic sources. Both types of coagulants are used in water treatment processes to help clarify and purify water by removing suspended particles and impurities.</p></p>
