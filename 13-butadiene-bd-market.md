<p><p>1,3-Butadiene (BD) is produced using various methods such as DMF (Dimethylformamide) Method, ACN (Acetonitrile) Method, and NMP (N-Methyl-2-pyrrolidone) Method. The DMF Method involves the dehydrogenation of butene in the presence of a DMF catalyst, while the ACN Method uses acrylonitrile as a co-feedstock. The NMP Method involves the pyrolysis of butane. Each method has its own advantages and results in different qualities of 1,3-Butadiene, catering to various industries such as synthetic rubber, plastics, and coatings.</p></p>
