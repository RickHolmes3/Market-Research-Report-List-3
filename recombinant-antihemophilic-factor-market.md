<p><p>Recombinant Antihemophilic Factor (rAHF) is a type of clotting factor medication used to treat and prevent bleeding in patients with hemophilia A, a genetic disorder that impairs blood clotting. It is produced through recombinant DNA technology, where the gene responsible for producing clotting factor VIII is inserted into host cells, such as Chinese hamster ovary cells, to produce a biologically active form of the clotting factor.</p><p>The Recombinant Antihemophilic Factor Market is witnessing significant growth due to several factors. The increasing prevalence of hemophilia A, the growing adoption of recombinant clotting factors over plasma-derived products, and the advantages of rAHF, such as reduced risk of transmitting bloodborne infections, are driving the market growth. Moreover, advancements in production techniques, such as cell culture technology, have improved the yield and purity of rAHF, further boosting its demand in the market.</p><p>In recent years, the market has been witnessing several trends that are likely to shape its growth in the coming years. One such trend is the development of extended half-life rAHF products, which allow for less frequent dosing and improved patient compliance. Companies are focusing on developing novel formulations with prolonged clotting factor activity, enhancing the convenience and efficacy of treatment.</p><p>Furthermore, there is a growing emphasis on the development of personalized therapies for hemophilia A, incorporating gene therapy and gene editing techniques. These advancements aim to provide long-lasting and potentially curative treatments for patients with hemophilia A, disrupting the traditional market landscape.</p><p>Overall, the Recombinant Antihemophilic Factor Market is expected to experience steady growth in the forecast period, with a projected CAGR of 7.3%.
