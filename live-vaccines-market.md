<p><p>Live vaccines are types of vaccines that contain weakened or attenuated virus or bacteria. Monovalent live vaccines are designed to protect against a single specific disease, such as measles or polio. On the other hand, multivalent live vaccines are formulated to protect against multiple diseases, combining different attenuated virus or bacteria strains. Both types of vaccines work by stimulating the immune system to produce a response, which results in long-lasting protection against the targeted diseases. They are effective in preventing infections and are commonly used in vaccination programs worldwide.</p></p>
