<p><p>Amalgam fillings are commonly used in dental restorations and fall into two main market types: Low-copper Amalgam and High Copper Amalgam. Low-copper Amalgam is a traditional formulation with less than 6% copper content, providing good durability and ease of use. High Copper Amalgam, on the other hand, contains over 12% copper, resulting in improved strength and corrosion resistance. Both types offer reliable and cost-effective options for restoring teeth damaged by decay or injury, with dentists selecting the appropriate type based on individual patient needs.</p></p>
