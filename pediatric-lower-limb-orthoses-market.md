<p><p>The Pediatric Lower Limb Orthoses Market includes two main types of orthotics: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide active support to the lower limbs, aiding in movement and maintaining proper alignment. They use springs, cables, or pneumatic systems to assist with walking and running. Static orthotics, on the other hand, are used to provide passive support and immobilization to the lower limbs. They are commonly used in situations where there is a need to stabilize or restrict movement, such as in cases of fractures or musculoskeletal disorders.</p></p>
