<p><p>Biological drugs, also known as biologics, are pharmaceutical products manufactured using biological sources such as living organisms or their components. Unlike traditional chemical-based drugs, biological drugs are highly targeted and specifically designed to interact with specific molecules or cells in the body to treat various diseases. They include vaccines, hormones, antibodies, and genetically engineered proteins.</p><p>The biological drugs market has been witnessing significant growth due to several factors. One of the key drivers is the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases such as cancer, diabetes, and autoimmune disorders. Biological drugs have shown promising results in managing and treating these conditions, fueling their demand. Furthermore, advancements in biotechnology and genetic engineering techniques have paved the way for the development of more sophisticated biological drugs with higher efficacy and improved patient outcomes.</p><p>Another factor driving market growth is the growing geriatric population worldwide. Elderly individuals are more susceptible to chronic diseases and require specialized treatments, often involving biological drugs. Moreover, the rising healthcare expenditure in both developed and developing nations is expected to contribute to market growth.</p><p>In terms of trends, the biological drugs market is witnessing a shift towards personalized medicine and precision therapies. This trend is expected to continue, driving market growth in the coming years.</p><p>Additionally, the market is witnessing a rise in the number of collaborations and partnerships between pharmaceutical companies and research institutes to develop novel biological drugs.
