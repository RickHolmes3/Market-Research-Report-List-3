<p><p>The term "5G" refers to the fifth generation of wireless technology, which promises faster and more reliable connectivity than its predecessors. In the context of autonomous vehicles, 5G plays a crucial role in enabling a wide array of advanced features and capabilities. With its ultra-low latency capabilities, 5G enables real-time vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) and vehicle-to-infrastructure (V2I) communication, creating a connected ecosystem for autonomous vehicles to operate efficiently and safely. This connectivity also enables the exchange of critical data, such as traffic conditions, road hazards, and real-time mapping, ultimately enhancing the overall autonomous driving experience.</p><p>Furthermore, 5G enables advanced vehicle-to-everything (V2X) communication, allowing autonomous vehicles to interact with pedestrians, cyclists, and other vulnerable road users.
